The Deep Released

"The dwarves did not merely uncover treasure beneath the mountain. They uncovered longing, memory, grief, and the Deep itself."

— Loryeras of Kel Alari

A historical account penned by Loryeras, royal historian of Kel Alari, some centuries after the Great Deluge.

About the Work

The Deep Released recounts the descent of King Ordrin of Bol Boldihr and his companions into the depths beneath the mountain in search of the legendary treasure associated with Thinrist and Thindor Firefist.

The work survives in fragmentary form and mixes translated inscriptions, oral histories, recovered dwarven accounts, and the commentary of Loryeras himself.

Much attention is given to the mysterious Singer discovered in the deep waters below the halls, and to the release of what later writings would call the Deep.

Authorship

Loryeras served as royal historian of Kel Alari several centuries after the Deluge.

His writings are among the most widely circulated surviving accounts concerning the fall of Bol Boldihr and the destruction that befell the valley.
